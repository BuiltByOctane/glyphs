# Code of Conduct

Glyph is a small project. These are the ground rules for participating here.

## What we expect

- **Be specific, not personal.** Critique code, designs, and decisions — not the people behind them. Disagree about an implementation freely; never about who someone is.
- **Assume the other person is acting in good faith.** Most friction in OSS is a misread tone, not malice. Ask before escalating.
- **Help newcomers land their first patch.** A good first PR experience is worth more than a perfect one.
- **Keep discussion in public.** Issues, PRs, and discussions are the record. Side-channel pressure on contributors or maintainers isn't acceptable.
- **Respect maintainer time.** "Bump?" comments and DMs after 24 hours don't help anyone. Maintainers are unpaid; reviews land when they land.

## What we don't tolerate

Harassment, discriminatory remarks, or sustained personal attacks — in issues, PRs, commits, code review, or anywhere else this project lives. The same goes for using Glyph's spaces (issue tracker, discussions, etc.) to dogpile, dox, or threaten anyone.

## Reporting

If you see behavior that violates this — or you're on the receiving end of it — email **innovationsoctane@gmail.com**. Reports are private. The maintainers will read every report, decide on a response, and follow up with you.

Plausible responses include: a private warning, a public correction, removing a comment or commit, temporary or permanent ban from the project's spaces. Severe or repeated violations skip straight to the latter.

## Scope

This applies to all project-controlled spaces (this repo, related discussions, project social accounts) and to anyone representing the project in public.

If something feels off and you're not sure if it crosses a line, email anyway. We'd rather hear about it.
